Steam power

 22nd December 2022 at 10:09am

As a steam-mage, you literally bring the heat: you have mastered the secret of efficiently transforming steam into magic. As a steam-mage, you can harness steam as an offensive or defensive tool, charge (or overcharge!) your spells, and go out with a bang if all else fails.

Arcane Craftsman

Beginning at 2nd level, you gain proficiency with padded armour, smith's tools, and the light hammer.

Boiler Backpack

You were taught (or have taught yourself) how to construct an insulated, portable steam furnace. Starting at 2nd level when you choose this school, you gain a boiler backpack. The boiler backpack is a miniature furnace, including two small hatches for fuel and water and a set of pipes. It can be used as an arcane focus. It weighs 20 pounds and reduces your speed by 10 ft. unless your Strength is 12 or higher.

Steam Jet

Beginning at 2nd level, while the boiler backpack is on your person, you can choose one of the following effects as a bonus action:

  • Slow release: You cast the fog cloud spell originating from your position. It lasts for 1d4 rounds.
  • Quick release: 'Until the start of your next turn, you are affected by both the Expeditious Retreat and Jump spells (i.e. +30 ft. movement speed and tripled jump distance).
  • Wide release: Until the start of your next turn, you gain resistance to fire and cold damage. After using this ability, you must take a short or long rest to use it again. Additionally, you can expend any spell slot to recharge it.
  • Overheat: Beginning at 6th level, whenever you deal damage with a fire, thunder, or electricity spell, you can opt to roll an extra amount of D6 damage dice up to your Proficiency bonus. All targets receive the increased damage, but if any of the dice comes up as 6, your boiler backpack ruptures, dealing all additional damage to yourself as well.
  • Steam Bomb: Beginning at 10th level, you have learned how to turn your boiler backpack into a terrible weapon in a pinch. As an action, you can take off your backpack and throw it at a square within sight up to 60 ft. away. The backpack then explodes, dealing 8d6 fire and 8d6 bludgeoning damage and knocking prone every creature in a 30-ft radius circle (reflex save to halve damage and avoid being knocked prone). After using this ability, you cannot use any spells other than cantrips until you rebuild you boiler backpack during a long rest.
